Which connection mode should I use?

Gateway (recommended) for your lab lockers and most shares: file ownership is always correct and you sign in with your plain username. Use Direct NAS only for the department or project shares that need it.

What do I sign in with?

Your lab account password, with the username in the format the mode expects. Gateway: just your username (for example kriengkraip). Direct NAS: SIRIRAJ\username.

It says access denied or wrong password

Double-check the sign-in format for your mode (plain username for Gateway, SIRIRAJ\username for Direct). The password is your normal lab account password. If it still fails, ask on Zulip.

My files show the wrong owner or I cannot write

Use Gateway mode. It connects through the SISP gateway, so ownership and permissions are always correct.

Windows says the drive letter is already in use

Pick a different drive letter in the Connect panel, or disconnect the existing mapping first (right-click it in File Explorer and choose Disconnect).

How do I connect on a Mac?

Use the MapDrive macOS app (see Get the app), or in Finder choose Go, then Connect to Server, and enter smb://192.168.0.25/sisplockers (swap in the share you want).

How do I reconnect after a restart?

Open the MapDrive app and connect again, or re-run the connect steps. You can set the app to start at login so your drives come back on their own.

Still stuck?

Post in the MapDrive Help channel on Zulip with the share name, the mode, and the exact error. Please do not post passwords.
